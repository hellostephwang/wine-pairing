Pairing

Chardonnay’s subtle nature is easily overwhelmed by pungent foods. Pour it alongside the flavor equivalent of a noisy action flick, and it’s totally lost. What you want are quiet flavors—mild, subtle foods that are not too spicy, not too pungent and not too acidic. Keep spice and flavor intensity low while accentuating creamy, buttery flavors and textures. This is a wine worthy of lobster.
